# Intake and iteration

## Before the three concepts

Infer answers from the prompt and files first. Ask only blocking questions, normally no more than three. High-impact unknowns include:

- Audience and occasion.
- Required speaking time or slide count.
- Whether content must be strictly source-bound or may include researched supplementation.
- Required institution/author information.
- Whether the deck is projected, submitted for reading, or both.
- Whether the default academic route is correct or the topic implies a theme-led pitch/poster route.
- Whether a logo, official brand system, or project visual identity is required.

If the user does not answer a non-blocking question, choose a sensible default and state it briefly.

## After the three contact sheets

Ask the user to select A, B, C, or a precise hybrid. Summarize each in one sentence and recommend one based on audience/occasion. If the user chooses a hybrid, confirm only unresolved conflicts—for example dark vs light canvas, serif vs sans title, restrained vs dense illustration. Do not reopen the storyline unless the user asks for content changes.

## After formal version 1

Ask 5–9 tailored questions in one organized set. Do not repeat known facts. Point to concrete choices seen in version 1 and provide a recommended default where helpful. Select from:

### Content emphasis

- Which section should receive more/less speaking time?
- Should the deck emphasize explanation, critique, comparison, or recommendation?
- Are any claims too strong, too cautious, or outside the user's intended position?

### Audience calibration

- Which concepts can be assumed known?
- Should technical details move to the appendix or be expanded in the main flow?
- Is the deck primarily spoken or read independently?

### Evidence and sources

- Are there required papers, datasets, internal documents, or figures still missing?
- Must citations follow a specific style?
- May the deck include newer external evidence, or only supplied material?

### Visual direction

- Keep the full YD header/footer identity or simplify it?
- Prefer denser teaching-handout pages or more spacious conference pages?
- Should navy/red remain fixed, or adapt to an institution palette?
- Are there figures that must remain editable or be redrawn?
- For a theme-led pitch, does the chosen visual world feel specific to the project rather than generically “cool”?
- For academic reconstruction, are any complex visual assets required to become separate image objects beyond the current layer split?
- For creative output, should any individual slide be regenerated at higher impact while preserving the chosen campaign style?

### Delivery constraints

- Exact duration, room/screen size, and whether printing is expected.
- Need for speaker notes, appendix, bilingual labels, or animations.

## Revision discipline

Convert answers into a change ledger. Resolve global design and narrative decisions first, then slide-level edits. Keep accepted pages intact. Re-render every slide after any change to font, theme, slide order, or layout constants.

Aim for version 2 to be submission-ready. Ask further questions only if the answers introduce a new conflict or missing high-impact source.
