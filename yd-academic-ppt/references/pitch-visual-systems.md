# Theme-led pitch visual systems

This reference governs the visual worlds and narrative expression of theme-led pitches. The default hackathon/creative final output keeps the selected full-slide images and follows `poster-impact-mode.md` for assembly. If the user explicitly requests editable creative output, reconstruct the selected concept with `image-to-editable-reconstruction.md` while preserving this visual system.

## Core principle

Do not recolor the academic template and call it a pitch. Derive a specific visual world from the product's premise, audience, venue, and emotional promise. Lock it in a private design system before building pages:

- central metaphor and hero object;
- background environment;
- palette roles;
- display/body typography;
- image style and perspective;
- chrome, frames, icon family, and repeated motifs;
- 4–6 layout variants;
- negative constraints;
- native-text safe zones.

Use the production chain `brief → storyline → design system → storyboard → generation/authoring → QA`. This borrows the disciplined planning insight of image-first pitch workflows without generating ordinary text into slide bitmaps.

## Route examples

### Youthful app / playful service

- Black or dark stage, high-saturation accent colors, bold sans-serif titles, sticker or Memphis motifs.
- One oversized metaphorical device/object per page.
- Short copy, strong verbs, expressive but controlled geometry.
- Avoid random rainbow decoration and unreadable micro-labels.

### Game / pixel / roguelike

- Build around the actual game world: pixel-industrial, fantasy, cozy, retro arcade, or another faithful art direction.
- Use environmental scenes, characters, portals, maps, inventory frames, or HUD-inspired chrome sparingly.
- Keep gameplay loop and screenshots faithful; use generated imagery for atmosphere, not fake gameplay proof.

### Cultural-tourism / historical experience

- Choose one coherent route: cinematic heritage, archival-paper collage, black-gold night journey, ink-and-neon fusion, etc.
- Combine authentic artifacts, location photography, maps, cards, and generated atmosphere with clear provenance.
- Avoid generic “Chinese style” symbols unrelated to the place or period.

### Technology / AI platform

- Use deep blue, electric cyan, violet, or product brand colors with restrained glow.
- Prefer architecture, data flow, capability map, and product evidence over empty futuristic effects.
- Keep technical diagrams and metrics native and exact.

### Coffee / food / lifestyle brand

- Derive palette and texture from the actual product: roasted brown, cream, copper, botanical green, fruit tones, packaging materials, or shop environment.
- Use product photography or generated still-life scenes with deliberate negative space.
- Avoid generic café stock photos and excessive rustic decoration unless the brand brief supports it.

## Layout behavior

- Cover: one powerful promise plus one hero visual.
- Problem/opportunity: emotionally legible scene or contrast, not a wall of bullets.
- Product/core loop: large interaction path with 3–6 clear steps.
- Demo: real screenshots in a coherent device/gallery frame.
- Differentiation: exact comparison or three decisive advantages.
- Technology: simplified architecture with credible implementation detail.
- Impact/business: native charts, metrics, users, partners, or outcomes.
- Closing: memorable promise, ask, roadmap, or call to action.

## QA for strong-style decks

- The visual system must feel specific to the project without overpowering content.
- Generated visual assets must share palette, lighting, perspective, and finish.
- Native text must look integrated rather than pasted over an image.
- Decorative density must not obscure hierarchy.
- The deck must still communicate when thumbnails are viewed in sequence.
- Strong cover and closing slides must not compensate for weak middle slides.
